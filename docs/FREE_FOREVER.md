# Free Forever

## Purpose

This document formalizes the commitment that Hackathon Foundation will **always remain free**. No paid AI models, no paid tools, no paid APIs, no premium tiers, no "pro" features, no subscriptions, no paywalls.

## Explanation

### What "Free Forever" means

| Category | Commitment |
|---|---|
| AI Models | Only free AI models are referenced, recommended, or used. DeepSeek, MiMo, Nemotron, Gemma, Qwen, Llama, GLM, Kimi, Gemini Flash — all free. |
| Tools | Only free tools are recommended. OpenCode, Gemini CLI, Continue, Cline, Roo Code, VS Code — all free. |
| APIs | Only free APIs are cataloged. No API key with a credit card required. |
| Hosting | Only free hosting platforms are listed. |
| Resources | Every icon set, font, image library, UI kit, and dataset is free to use. |
| Repository | The repository itself is open source under the MIT License. |

### What "Free Forever" does NOT mean

- It does not mean low quality. Free tools in 2026 are powerful enough for professional work.
- It does not mean "no budget." It means the framework itself imposes no cost. Users can spend money on their own infrastructure if they choose.
- It does not mean anti-commercial. Contributors can use these patterns in commercial projects. The framework just doesn't require any payment.

## Why it exists

### Accessibility

Most students and developers in the world cannot afford paid AI subscriptions. The median monthly income in many countries makes a $20/month AI subscription a significant expense. Hackathon Foundation removes this barrier entirely.

### Constraint-driven innovation

Free is not a limitation — it is a design constraint that forces better decisions. When you cannot throw money at the problem, you must be creative, systematic, and efficient. The framework that emerges from this constraint is stronger.

### Trust

A "Free Forever" commitment is rare and valuable. It tells every user that this repository exists to help, not to monetize. It builds the trust necessary for community adoption and contribution.

### Alignment with open source

The MIT License already guarantees the code is free. The "Free Forever" commitment extends this principle to every resource, tool, and recommendation in the repository.

## Long-term vision

1. **The repository is self-sustaining through community contribution, not revenue.** There is no business model. There is no monetization strategy. The project succeeds if it helps people.
2. **"Free Forever" becomes a recognized brand.** When developers see Hackathon Foundation, they immediately trust that everything inside it costs nothing.
3. **The framework proves that free AI is sufficient.** By demonstrating excellence using only free tools, the project challenges the assumption that paid AI is necessary for quality.

## Future scalability

As the AI ecosystem evolves, maintaining "Free Forever" requires active curation:

- When a previously free model becomes paid, it is removed and replaced.
- When a new free tool emerges, it is evaluated and added.
- When a paid service offers a generous free tier, it may be included with clear documentation of its free limits.

The commitment is to the *constraint*, not to any specific tool. Tools change. The principle does not.

---

> **Hackathon Foundation is free forever.**
