# Mission

## Why Hackathon Foundation exists

Hackathon Foundation exists to solve a fundamental problem: **most developers using AI coding assistants produce inconsistent, low-quality, and unmaintainable code because they treat AI as a text generator rather than an engineering team.**

The framework solves this by treating the repository as a software company. The user is the CEO. AI coding assistants are employees with defined roles, responsibilities, rules, and expected outputs.

## Who it serves

### Students

Students often have the passion and ideas but lack the engineering discipline to execute them professionally. Hackathon Foundation provides the structure that experienced engineers have learned through years of practice — but packaged in a way that anyone can use.

### Hackathon participants

Hackathons are chaotic. Teams form, ideas shift, and code piles up. Under time pressure, quality collapses. The framework provides a repeatable structure that reduces chaos, allowing participants to focus on building instead of organizing.

### Self-taught developers

Not everyone has access to professional engineering mentorship. The framework encodes professional engineering practices into a reusable structure that anyone can learn from and apply.

### Experienced engineers

Even seasoned engineers benefit from a structured framework for AI collaboration. It reduces context-switching, enforces consistency across sessions, and provides a reliable process for delivering quality work under time constraints.

## Long-term vision

The long-term vision is a world where:

1. **Every hackathon project starts with structure.** The default approach is to clone a framework, not to start from zero.
2. **AI-assisted development produces professional-quality results.** The structure of the framework, not the capability of the AI model, determines the quality ceiling.
3. **Free tools are sufficient for professional work.** The framework proves that orchestration and discipline matter more than expensive AI subscriptions.
4. **The framework is community-owned and community-improved.** It evolves through contributions from the people who use it.

## Student-first philosophy

Hackathon Foundation prioritizes students in every decision:

- **Free.** No student should need a paid subscription to use the framework.
- **Beginner-friendly.** The framework assumes no prior experience with AI-assisted development.
- **Educational.** Every part of the framework teaches engineering best practices.
- **Accessible.** Works with any operating system, any editor, any AI tool.

## Free Forever philosophy

The Free Forever philosophy is a non-negotiable constraint on every decision:

- Only free AI models are cataloged and recommended.
- Only free tools are integrated and documented.
- Only free hosting, APIs, and resources are listed.
- The repository itself is MIT-licensed.

See `docs/FREE_FOREVER.md` for the full commitment.

## Connection to the company culture

This mission shapes everything in the company. Every role, every policy, every asset exists to serve students and hackathon participants. See [company-culture.md](./company-culture.md) for the values that bring this mission to life.
