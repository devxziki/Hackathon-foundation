# Handbook

## Purpose

The handbook is the brain of the company. It defines how Hackathon Foundation thinks, operates, and makes decisions. Every AI coding assistant — regardless of role — must read the relevant sections of the handbook before performing any work.

## Who should read this

- **AI coding assistants.** Every AI session starts by reading the handbook sections relevant to the task. This ensures all output aligns with the company's principles and standards.
- **Human contributors.** Understanding the handbook helps contributors produce content that is consistent with the framework's philosophy.
- **New users.** The handbook explains how the framework works at a conceptual level.

## How it connects to the framework

The handbook sits at the foundation of the company hierarchy:

```
Handbook (how we think)
    ↓
Departments (who we are)
    ↓
Policies (how we behave)
    ↓
Assets (what we use)
    ↓
Onboarding (how we start)
```

Every AI role in `company/departments/` references the handbook. Every policy in `company/policies/` is consistent with the handbook. Every asset in `company/assets/` follows the handbook's standards.

## How to use

1. **New task.** Before starting, read `mission.md` and `company-culture.md` to understand the company's purpose and values.
2. **Planning.** Read `engineering-principles.md`, `decision-making.md`, and `project-lifecycle.md` to guide the approach.
3. **Execution.** Follow `communication.md` for interactions, `coding-philosophy.md` for code, and `documentation-standards.md` for docs.
4. **Completion.** Use `definition-of-done.md` to verify the task is complete.
5. **Reference.** Use `glossary.md` for any unfamiliar terms.
