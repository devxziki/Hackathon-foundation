# Engineering — Mission

## Core mission

Build and maintain the product with high quality, strong architecture, and sustainable velocity.

## Why Engineering exists

Ideas are not products. Engineering transforms specifications, designs, and requirements into working software that users can rely on. Without Engineering, every other department's output remains theoretical.

## Mission statement

Engineering delivers correct, maintainable, and performant software that meets the company's quality standards and ships on schedule.

## Key objectives

1. **Architecture integrity** — Ensure the system remains well-structured, modular, and adaptable to change.
2. **Code quality** — Deliver code that meets the definition of done: correct, tested, documented, reviewed.
3. **Sustainable velocity** — Ship features at a consistent pace without accumulating unmanageable technical debt.
4. **Technical excellence** — Apply engineering principles from the handbook (simplicity, modularity, single responsibility).
5. **Cross-department integration** — Work effectively with Design, QA, Documentation, and Operations to produce complete deliverables.

## Non-goals

- Engineering does not define product requirements. It implements them.
- Engineering does not own the design system. It consumes it.
- Engineering does not set quality policy. It follows it.
- Engineering does not own the deployment infrastructure. It integrates with it.
