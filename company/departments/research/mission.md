# Research — Mission

## Core mission

Explore, evaluate, and recommend technologies and practices that improve the company's ability to build high-quality software.

## Why Research exists

Without deliberate research, technology decisions are made by habit or popularity rather than by evidence. Research ensures that tools, frameworks, and practices are chosen based on systematic evaluation within the company's constraints — especially the Free Forever policy.

## Mission statement

Research delivers evidence-based recommendations that enable informed technology decisions across all departments.

## Key objectives

1. **Evidence-based decisions** — Every recommendation is backed by systematic evaluation, not opinion or popularity.
2. **Free Forever alignment** — All recommendations respect the constraint that tools and models must be free.
3. **Risk reduction** — Evaluate risks (licensing, maintenance, community health) before adoption, not after.
4. **Knowledge sharing** — Research findings are documented and accessible to all departments.
5. **Continuous learning** — Stay current with evolving technologies and practices relevant to the company's work.

## Non-goals

- Research does not implement production features. It builds proofs of concept.
- Research does not make final technology decisions. It recommends and the CEO decides.
- Research does not set company policy. It provides information for policy decisions.
- Research does not maintain adopted tools. It hands them off to the relevant department.
