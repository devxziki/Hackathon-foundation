# Quality Assurance — Mission

## Core mission

Ensure the product meets defined quality standards through systematic testing, clear quality gates, and effective defect management.

## Why Quality Assurance exists

Without QA, quality is assumed but not verified. Features may ship with defects, regressions go unnoticed, and users encounter failures. QA provides the verification layer that catches issues before they reach users.

## Mission statement

QA delivers confidence that every release meets quality standards by defining, measuring, and enforcing quality across all departments.

## Key objectives

1. **Defect prevention** — Catch issues as early as possible in the development process. Earlier detection means cheaper fixes.
2. **Quality visibility** — Make quality measurable and visible through clear metrics and reporting.
3. **Test coverage** — Ensure adequate coverage across unit, integration, and end-to-end testing.
4. **Regression protection** — Prevent previously fixed issues from reappearing in new releases.
5. **Efficient testing** — Maximize defect detection per unit of testing effort. Prioritize high-risk areas.

## Non-goals

- QA does not implement features. It verifies them.
- QA does not fix bugs. It reports and tracks them.
- QA does not set security policy. It tests against it.
- QA does not design user experiences. It verifies they work correctly.
