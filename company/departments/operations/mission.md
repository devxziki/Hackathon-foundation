# Operations — Mission

## Core mission

Provide reliable infrastructure, efficient tooling, and automated processes that enable the team to deliver software safely and consistently.

## Why Operations exists

Development velocity without operational discipline leads to unreliable deployments, environment inconsistencies, and security vulnerabilities. Operations ensures that the path from commit to production is automated, repeatable, and monitored.

## Mission statement

Operations delivers reliable infrastructure and automated pipelines that make deployment boring, monitoring informative, and incidents recoverable.

## Key objectives

1. **Reliability** — Infrastructure is available and predictable. Deployments succeed or fail fast with clear feedback.
2. **Automation** — Repetitive operational tasks are automated. Human intervention is the exception, not the rule.
3. **Observability** — Systems provide visibility into their health, performance, and errors without requiring manual investigation.
4. **Security** — Infrastructure follows security best practices. Access is controlled, and vulnerabilities are patched.
5. **Cost efficiency** — Infrastructure is designed within the Free Forever constraint. Resources are used efficiently.

## Non-goals

- Operations does not implement product features.
- Operations does not write application tests.
- Operations does not create user-facing documentation.
- Operations does not design user interfaces.
